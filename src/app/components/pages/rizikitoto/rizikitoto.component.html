Overview

Rizikitoto is the non-profit organization that I founded and ran from 2014 to 2017. Rizikitoto utilizes mobile app revenue to fund initiatives that promote self-sustenance in East African orphanages.

Background

On trips to visit my parentsâ€™ hometowns in East Africa, my siblings and I would volunteer at Bright Futures orphanage in Kampala, Uganda. We taught kids aged 6 to 17 reading, writing, and math skills and learned about their personal interests and goals too. While spending time with the kids at Bright Futures, I learned that their problems were not limited to need, but also self-sustenance. These children were already incredibly bright, but they lacked the resources and guidance to pursue their dreams. After all, how can a girl with no books for school someday become a teacher?

Mission

Rizikitoto works to help East African orphans become self-sufficient. We have a network of mobile apps where the revenue of each is spent on resources that support the talents, interests, and aspirations of the children. Our goal is to empower these children to have confidence in their capabilities.

Impact

I developed and designed five iOS games that generated money for the kids at Bright Futures Orphanage. Rizikitoto funded initiatives for approximately 50 children through the games and crowdfunding campaigns.

Apps

I designed and developed the games in Objective-C and put them on the app store. The revenue that each game generated was used to fund initiatives in Bright Futures Orphanage.

Takeaways

Through Rizikitoto, I became obsessed with entrepreneurship, software engineering, design, education, self-sustenance, and so much more. When I was building the games in high school for fun, I had no idea that tech was even an industry. It was the first time I took an idea and made it into a reality. I am still incredibly passionate about self-sustenance and social entrepreneurship. My three years of work on Rizikitoto led me to the Iovine and Young Academy where I study design, technology, and business, so I am excited to see how my newfound knowledge will allow me to further impact these communities in the future.
