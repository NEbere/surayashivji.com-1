Overview

Mello is a project I’m working on with my roommate Jamie Haberman. Starting out as a team of two, we have been able to have our hands in the user & market research, design, tech and business aspects of this ongoing project. Our current team consists of Jamie and myself leading design and software, Eric Deng leading hardware, Caitlyn Clabaugh leading machine learning, and Colleen Quinn leading aromatherapy R&D.

Mello is an intelligent diffuser for the home that motivates a lifestyle of wellness for young adults through personalized aromatherapy. Mello leverages artificial intelligence to mechanically create blends in real time based on the user’s changing moods and stress factors.

Last semester was crazy. I was trying to find a job, I was staying up all night studying for exams, and I was working. I was overwhelmed. It gave me stress. Like so many other things will throughout my life and every other person’s life.

When I got home after a long night of studying during finals, I noticed a scent that made me feel relieved and calm without even having to do anything. It was coming from my roommate Jamie’s room, who explained that it was lavender.

There are so many ways to deal with the high demands of day to day life, but it’s hard to incorporate them consistently. We thought, wouldn’t it be beneficial if our bodies received the right benefits at the right times throughout the day?

Meet Mello.

Mello is an intelligent diffuser for the home that motivates a lifestyle of wellness for young adults through personalized aromatherapy. Mello leverages AI to mechanically create blends in real time based on the user’s changing moods and stress factors.

This is a render of the Mello diffuser in a bedroom.

When the users buys Mello, they can simply spring load the oils into the six slots, which are designed to only accept our oils.

The user connects their Mello to our app.

They onboard by answering a few questions about their moods, stress levels, and needs at that moment. The intelligent algorithm uses that information to curate personalized blends. We came up with the idea of personalizing aromatherapy because many young adults in our research saw benefit in the practice, but didn’t understand how it directly relates them.

The user can learn about why each oil was selected for them and Mello does all the work by blending and diffusing based on their schedule.

Mello can be controlled by smart watches and voice-operated by smart devices. This connected experience allows Mello to tap into your heart rate, calendar, schedule, and more.

Adopting the Razor Blade Model, we have a fixed revenue stream for the diffuser, which we are selling for $15 a unit at a 20% margin. Pricing the diffuser at the Average Selling Price of $60 is unrealistic for our segment. So, we are selling Mello at a low price that is affordable for our target audience. The key is our recurring revenue stream, the essential oils, which we are charging users $10/month at an 83% margin.

Based on the 3.4 trillion dollar wellness market, consumers are clearly willing to spend on personal wellness. Within this vertical, the 240 m dollar segment of young adults is an under-served category that, based on our research, has a high growth rate. Thus, we are building this niche product to meet the growing and unmet demand of these wellness-conscious young adults.

Our target customers are 18-35 years old who care about health and aesthetics. They are tech savvy and have busy schedules. Over the past few months, we have interviewed over 300 people in our target market to understand what matters to them most. These conversations allowed us to segment our market further. At their core, our target customers crave low-effort, aesthetically pleasing experiences.

Our customers and product align with the market trends of smart home products, wellness and personalized consumer goods.

We’ve outlined offline and online strategies for how we’ll reach our customers. Because our target audience values authentic, photo-worthy experiences, we’ve included pop ups, influencers, and social.

Initially, we will focus on partnerships, contests, and social because they are low cost, large reach initiatives.

Our direct competitors include scent based companies like Muji, Pium, Saje and Glade. Clearly, when it comes to aromatherapy, we’re far more technically advanced.

Which is why we have competitors in home devices like Google Home, Nest, and Amazon Alexa, who have never explored scent

While current home products focus on technological and voice innovation, Mello believes the home should care for you. The future of Mello is a suite of products for the home that focus on personalized, data-driven, and passive wellness. For the Mello diffuser, our algorithm maps personal data to aroma; in the future we will apply our same algorithm to things like perfume, shampoo, and soap. We could also move into business. Imagine you’re traveling to a new country and as you walk into your hotel room there’s a Mello that’s diffusing the blends that it knows work for you.
